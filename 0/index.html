Project 0: Becoming Friends with Your Camera

CS180: Intro to Computer Vision and Computational Photography

University of California, Berkeley

Overview

This project explores the relationship between perspective, focal length/zoom, and the center of projection through practical photography experiments. Through three distinct parts, we investigate how camera positioning and settings affect image composition and perception.

Part 1: Selfie - The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

Close-up selfie - Notice the distorted facial features from being too close

Taken from further away with zoom - Natural facial proportions preserved

Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

Zoomed in from far away - Notice how the buildings appear flattened and compressed

Closer view without zoom - Natural depth perspective preserved

Part 3: The Dolly Zoom

The "Vertigo effect" - Moving backward while zooming in keeps the subject the same size while the background dramatically changes
